Grilled chicken

Description

Although Brazilian lunches most often contain red meat, chicken is a widely used option for non-vegetarian diets that still aim to reduce the carbon footprint. Grilled chicken is also widely sold on small meat skewers grilled over an open fire, a very popular street snack in Brazil.

Ingredients

  • 3 chicken breast fillets
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil

Steps

  1. In a bowl, rub the garlic and salt into the chicken fillets.
  2. Drizzle a skillet with olive oil, place it over medium heat, and brown the fillets.
  3. Lower the heat and cook for 5 minutes on each side.
